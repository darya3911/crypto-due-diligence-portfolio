## 1. Team

I always start with people. In a script, this is the most important role—a candidate can be copied, a team is either there or not.

And then a surprise awaited me. BlockDAG is one of those conservative projects that doesn't hide behind monkey avatars. I found and scrolled through the LinkedIn profiles of almost all the famous people.

- **Anthony Turner (CEO)** — former COO of a Swiss publicly traded company. His profile is active, with a history and references.
- **Jeremy Harkness (CTO)** — experience in artificial intelligence and mid-tier distribution.
- **Youssef Khaulaj (CSO)** — Head of Security.
- **Stephen Clark** — Senior Partnership Advisor.
- **Allen Joseph** — Head of Developer Relations.

And now for the most interesting part. **Dr. Maurice Herlihy (Blockchain Consultant)** — MIT professor, Gödel and Dijkstra Prize winner. In short: it's like the Nobel Prize for computer science. People like that don't get involved in junk projects—reputation is more important.

But digging deeper, I found something strange. People are writing some not-so-nice things in the comments section. Someone asks, "Why did you lie about the exchange announcement on June 13th?" Someone else asks, "What about the partnership with the NBA?" And then there's silence.

**Red Flags:**
- Anonymity is fine—everyone is public.
- No fake subscribers are visible.
- But communication with the community is a serious problem.

**My Notes:**
The team is strong, especially the advisors. But either the project has outgrown its capabilities, or someone messed up the communications. Ignoring community questions is a bad strategy before listing.

## 2. Product

**What does the project do?**

BlockDAG is a layer 1 hybrid DAG (directed acyclic graph) and Proof-of-Work. Simply put, they're trying to make it run smoothly when there are a lot of users.

Traditional networks like Bitcoin and Ethereum process transactions constantly—like queues at a store. BlockDAG promises to do this in parallel while maintaining security.

**Is there a working product?**

Yes, and that's a plus. The **live testnet (Primordial Testnet)** is operational. I've logged in:
- There's a public explorer
- A faucet with test tokens (you can get them and play)
- You can deploy contracts

This is no longer just an idea on paper.

**Roadmap and Delays**

The official document (April 2024) outlines the development plan. But the mainnet was supposed to launch in Q3 2025, and the launch has been delayed.

**My Notes:**
The technology looks interesting—they're genuinely trying to create something new, not just copy-pasting malware. Delays? This happens all the time in crypto. It's better to release a working product later than a half-baked one right away. But a delay + community disregard = a wake-up call.
## 3. Tokenomics

**General Information:**
- Ticker: BDAG
- Blockchain: Native Network (EVM-compatible)
- Total Supply: **150 billion coins**

**Token Distribution:**

| Purpose | Billions | Percentage |
|------------|-----------|----------|
| Miners | 75 | 50% |
| Presale | 50 | 33.3% |
| Community and Ecosystem | 19 | 12.7% |
| Liquidity | 4.5 | 3% |
| Team | 1.5 | 1% |

Figures that make you take a closer look:

1. **50% to miners** is correct for a PoW network. They don't take everything for themselves, but give it to the community.
2. **The presale raised $449 million**—a record. People are bringing money, which means they believe.
3. **The team only has 1%**—and that's the key. Usually, I see 20-30% for the team, which they can sell immediately after the listing. Here, the team's tokens are **locked for 2 years**.

**What is the token for?**
- Transaction fees
- Mining (PoW)
- Staking (planned)
- Access to dApps in the ecosystem

**My notes:**
The tokenomics are one of the most honest I've seen recently. 1% for the team with a 2-year lock suggests the founders are in it for the long haul. If they were planning a scam, the distribution would be completely different.

## 4. Security

**Audits:**
- **CertiK** — yes, there is
- **Halborn** — also yes

Two top firms have audited the contracts. CertiK and Halborn don't work with outright garbage projects—they value their licenses.

**What's in the reports:**

According to the project, auditors checked the vesting contracts and PoW logic. Critical vulnerabilities were fixed before launch.

**Contract:**
- Verified by scanner (for testnet)
- No suspicious functions like `mint` or `blacklist` found
- A public bug bounty program is active

**My notes:**
Two audits from top firms is expensive (hundreds of thousands of dollars). For a scam project, such expenses are pointless. They're clearly preparing for the long game.

## 6. My Verdict

**Brief Summary**

BlockDAG is an ambitious Layer-1 project with strong technology (a DAG+PoW hybrid), a public team (including an MIT professor), two top-tier audits, and real Formula 1-level partnerships. The tokenomics are among the most honest I've seen.

**Potential**

- Huge community (200k+ holders, 2.5M+ X1 users)
- Strong partnerships for mass advertising
- Fair tokenomics without team greed
- A functioning testnet is not air

**Risks**

- Delays in the mainnet launch
- Negativity in the community due to unfulfilled promises (exchanges, NBA)
- High expectations after a record-breaking presale – easy to disappoint the market
- ​​Accusations of scam (but facts suggest it's more likely FUD)

**Recommendation for listing**

I would say this: **the project is worth considering**, but with two conditions:

1. The team urgently needs to establish communication with the community. Silence kills.

2. Closely monitor the first days after the listing – will presale investors dump everything at once?

This project is too promising to pass by. But there's too much noise around to just buy it blindly.
